Slay ("the app", "we", "us") is built around a simple idea: a private daily check-in that's yours. This policy explains what data the app uses, where it lives, and the choices you have. We keep data collection to the minimum needed to make the app work, and we never sell your data.

Who we are

Slay is published by XFour. If you have any questions about this policy or your data, contact us at privacy@xfour.co.uk.

What the app stores

Your daily check-ins. Each time you log a check-in, the app saves your slay-ness score and your genderality value, along with the date. These entries are stored locally on your device and, if you are signed in to iCloud, synced through Apple's iCloud (CloudKit) under your own Apple ID. Your profile. If you use the friends features, the app publishes a small profile so friends can find you and see your latest check-in. This profile includes your display name, your friend code, your most recent slay-ness and genderality, the time you last updated, and — only if you choose to add it — your phone number. This is stored in the app's iCloud (CloudKit) database, which is tied to your Apple ID. Your settings. Preferences such as your display name and reminder time are stored on your device.

Contacts

If you choose to "Find friends from contacts", the app reads your contacts on your device only to check which of your contacts are already on Slay. Your contacts are not uploaded to us or anyone else and are not stored off your device. Only normalized phone numbers are compared locally. You can decline contacts access and still use the app; it simply won't suggest contacts you already know.

Notifications

With your permission, the app sends you a daily reminder to check in and, if you enable besties, a notification when a close friend updates their check-in. You can turn notifications off at any time in iOS Settings.

How your data is shared

  • With friends you add. When someone adds you using your friend code (or finds you via contacts), they can see your display name and your current slay-ness and genderality. They cannot see your full history.
  • We do not sell your data. Ever.
  • No advertising or tracking. Slay contains no ads, no analytics SDKs, and no third-party trackers. We do not track you across other apps or websites.

Where your data lives and who can access it

Your entries and profile are stored on your device and in iCloud (CloudKit) under your own Apple ID. This means your data is handled by Apple's iCloud infrastructure subject to Apple's terms and privacy policy. We, as the developer, do not run a separate server that collects or stores your personal entries.

Your choices and rights

  • You can delete any check-in from your history at any time.
  • You can remove friends, and stop publishing your profile by not using the friends features.
  • You can revoke contacts and notifications permissions in iOS Settings.
To delete your shared profile data, remove the app and sign out of/ delete the relevant iCloud data, or contact us at privacy@xfour.co.uk and we will help.

Children

Slay is not directed at children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us information, contact privacy@xfour.co.uk.

Changes to this policy

If we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date above and, where appropriate, notify you in the app.
